Computational & Applied Mathematics (CAM) Seminar

Friday, 2pm - 4pm, Location: Lecture Room 4, Floor 2, Jin Chun Yuan West Building

Title: Numerical Relativity and Gravitational Wave detection

Speaker: Cao Zhoujian, CAS, （曹周键，中科院数学所）

Time: Mar. 11

Abstract： The sucess of GW150914 is a combination the breakthroughs of experimental technologies and theoretical research. The effective one body numerical relativity model (EOBNR) played a key role in the data analysis. It diged out the data envoloped in the noise and realized 5.1 sigma confidence level. And more it recoginized the gravitational wave source is a merging binary black hole system. In this talk I will introduce how the theoretical model can do such a powerful work. After that I will introduce the related issues in the numerical relativity.
